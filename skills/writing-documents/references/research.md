# Writing Research Documents

Synthesis without prescription.

## Purpose

Research informs decisions without making them. Present facts, comparisons, and synthesis. Let the reader decide.

## Structure

```markdown
# [Topic] Research

Research for [what decision this informs].

## Context

[What decision this research informs. Why now. What's in scope, what's out.]

---

## [Option/Area A]

[Facts, capabilities, limitations]

[Field sentiment if relevant—adoption, community preferences]

> [Source](link) — what it establishes

## [Option/Area B]

[Facts, capabilities, limitations]

> [Source](link) — what it establishes

---

## Comparison

| Dimension | Option A | Option B |
|-----------|----------|----------|
| [Metric] | [Value] | [Value] |

---

## Gaps

- [What you couldn't determine]
- [What needs expert input]
- [What may be stale]

---

## Summary

[Brief synthesis—no recommendations]
```

## The Line

**Synthesis** (acceptable):
- "Option A is 3x faster in benchmarks"
- "Option B has broader community adoption"
- "Option A requires less configuration"

**Prescription** (not research):
- "You should use Option A"
- "Option B is the better choice"
- "We recommend Option A"

Synthesis states facts and relationships. Prescription tells the reader what to do. Research stays on the synthesis side.

## Principles

**Purpose first**: If you don't know what decision this informs, ask before writing.

**Sources near claims**: Every factual claim needs a source. Put sources near the claims they support, not collected at the end.

**Gaps explicit**: What you couldn't determine is as important as what you could. Hidden gaps mislead.

**Dates matter**: Source freshness affects validity. Note when sources were published.

## Test

Could someone with different values reach a different conclusion from the same research? If yes, you've stayed neutral. If no, prescription crept in.

## Anti-patterns

| Pattern | Problem |
|---------|---------|
| Hidden recommendations | Becomes advocacy |
| Sources at end | Hard to verify claims |
| Undated sources | Stale info misleads |
| Hidden gaps | Creates false confidence |
| No purpose statement | Research without direction |
