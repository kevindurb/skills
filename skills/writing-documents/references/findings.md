# Writing Findings Documents

Answer the question. Answer before evidence.

## Purpose

Findings documents answer specific questions with evidence. The answer comes first—readers shouldn't have to wade through methodology to find what they need.

## Structure

```markdown
# [Question]?

## Answer

[Direct answer in 1-3 sentences. No hedging.]

## Summary

[Key points that support the answer. Bullets, not prose.]

---

## Evidence

### [Evidence Category 1]

[What was examined]

[What was found]

> [Source or method]

### [Evidence Category 2]
...

---

## Confidence

[How confident you are and why. What would change the answer.]

## Gaps

[What you couldn't determine. What further investigation would reveal.]
```

## Principles

**Answer first**: The reader has a question. Answer it immediately. Everything else supports that answer.

**No speculation**: If you don't know, say so. Speculation disguised as findings causes bad decisions.

**Sources near evidence**: Each finding should be traceable to how you know it.

**Confidence explicit**: "We're 90% sure because X" is more useful than unstated certainty.

**No unsolicited recommendations**: Findings answer questions. Recommendations belong elsewhere.

## Test

After reading the first two sections (Answer + Summary), does the reader have what they need? The evidence section is for verification, not comprehension.

## Anti-patterns

| Pattern | Problem |
|---------|---------|
| Answer buried | Reader has to dig |
| Hedged answers | Creates confusion |
| Speculation as fact | Misleads decisions |
| Missing confidence | False certainty |
| Recommendations | Scope creep |
| Methodology first | Buries the answer |
